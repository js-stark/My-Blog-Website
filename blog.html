Magnetic Shielding of Underground Cables

Sai Anirudh Date July 13, 2018 4

Urbanisation is one of the best things that ever happened after the 18th century. I’m sure that many people will not concur with my viewpoint and I am also sure that most of them will have strong roots in rural areas. Fair enough. Most people have strong roots with their place of birth and it is something that is quite understandable. The point that I am trying to convey is that urbanisation brought along with it has many benefits that were completely unheard of. It presented better access to education, better jobs, better standards of living, better hospitals, etc. There is however one unwelcoming facet of urbanisation that chugged along naturally, something that looks innocuous on first sight but has tonnes of ugly issues on its wayside. Yes, I am talking about a monumental increase in power requirement. People in thick urban areas are no strangers to energy sucking structures like huge malls, big apartments, tourist sites of attraction, etc. Most of them guzzle power like crazy. In order to meet the high power demand, a strong need to transport power from far off power stations through Underground Cables was felt as it is difficult to transmit power using overhead lines within city limits. It is equally difficult to set up a powerplant in close proximity to the outer peripheries of a city. Thus, with no other go, electrical engineers of the yesteryears had to make merry with the prospects of transmitting power inside the city using Underground Cables. While it might look as if it is safe to transmit power using underground cables, one might do well to realise that such ideas are quite far from the truth. The biggest trouble with underground cables is that the magnetic field produced by the currents carried by them is felt quite strongly on the surface of the Earth. It is quite dangerous to be continuously exposed to such high magnetic fields on an everyday basis and therein lies our biggest problem. Only a handful of people are aware of the hazards posed by underground cables and it is quite difficult to convince others about the risks associated with it.

One of the methodologies of dealing with it is by employing magnetic shields. Magnetic shields perform a terrific job at mitigating magnetic fields established by underground cables. There are two main ways of achieving our objective. One is by using a material of high permeability, thereby allowing most of the flux lines of the magnetic field to go through them, as opposed to going above them. By doing this, we are reducing the field produced by the underground cables on the surface of the Earth. The second method is by strategically placing a passive loop over the underground cables so that when magnetic flux lines attempt to flow through them, by Faraday’s laws, it produces its own magnetic field that opposes the original one, thereby opposing it. We are, in a way, deleting the original field. From a commercial standpoint, the second methodology is usually prefered. There are many products already available in the market that are based on the concept of cancelling the original magnetic field. A lot of research work is going on with the first method but we are a bit far from practically realising the idea. I am going to give a concise gist on both of the aforementioned methods

First up, in the category of using materials of high permeability, there are three types of shields available, them being a horizontal based shield, reverse U based shield and a closed box shield. One point to be noted here is that in all the cases, the shield must be placed right above the cable and not near the soil, a simple mistake that many people make on first glance. Once placed over the cables, these absorb most of the flux lines produced by the cables, thereby allowing only a small amount to reach the surface of the soil. It is reasonably effective in reducing the magnetic field produced by Underground cables. Aluminium and Steel are the materials that are normally preferred while making the shield. Both achieve a very good reduction factor. Aluminium has a conductivity of 3.8*10^7 S/m and a relative permeability of 1 while Steel has a conductivity of 10^7 S/m and a relative permeability of 500

One drawback with this is that it allows magnetic flux lines to escape along the sides. This is why we started using reverse U shaped magnetic shields. They are very similar to horizontal plate based shields but also prevent flux lines from escaping along the sides. This allows it to have a reduction factor that is slightly better than that with a horizontal plate.Other materials apart from Alumenium and Steel are tested, them being Ferrite and Ni-Fe. The graph of their shielding efficiencies is obtained

It can further be reduced by housing the whole structure inside a metallic box, thereby providing the flux lines with virtually no means of escaping out of the complex. It is the most efficient way of shielding the surface of the soil from the magnetic fields of underground cables. One slight drawback with it is that it causes the wire to heat up. So, to prevent the wires from heating up, we must reduce the magnitude of current that is flowing through the wires. This hinders with our ability to send currents of high magnitude using underground cablesClearly, we can observe that a closed shield performs best when it comes to mitigating magnetic fields of UGCs. Methods of optimising the shielding efficiencies was also studied and it was found that by increasing the length of the horizontal plate, the magnetic field can be drastically reducedThe second method of magnetic shielding involves using loops. The loop is placed over the underground cables. As the magnetic field of the underground cables manages to penetrate through the loops, by Faraday’s law, a current will flow through the loop such that it opposes the change in the magnetic field. So the newly established magnetic field opposes the existing magnetic field and thus the field does not reach the soil. We are in a way, “deleting” the existing magnetic field. In the industry level, this is the most popular method of magnetically shielding the underground cables at the moment. Sometimes, a magnetic core can be added to the loops to enhance its field cancellation capabilities. This method does a terrific job at improving the overall efficiency of magnetic shielding.
